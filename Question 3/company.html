Question One

  • Initial Consultation:

    Schedule a meeting with the company to understand their requirements,
    goals, and target audience. Discuss their brand identity, desired features, and any specific design preferences.
    Gather all necessary information about the company and its products or services.

  • Define Scope and Objectives:

    Based on the initial consultation, clearly define the scope of the project.
    Outline the website's functionality, key pages, and any additional features or integrations required.
    Set specific objectives and determine the project's timeline and budget.

  • Planning and Wireframing:

    Create a sitemap that outlines the website's structure and navigation.
    Develop wireframes or sketches to visualize the layout and placement of key elements on each page.
    This step helps in establishing a clear website hierarchy and user flow.

  • Design and Mockups:

    Create a visual design for the website using graphic design tools or website builders.
    Consider the company's branding guidelines, color scheme, typography, and overall aesthetics.
    Design key pages such as the homepage, product/service pages, contact page, etc., and create mockups for client approval.

  • Content Creation:

    Work with the company to gather content for the website, including text, images,
    videos, and other media. Ensure that the content aligns with the company's branding and effectively communicates their message.
    Optimize the content for search engines (SEO) if required.

  • Development:

    Once the design and content are approved, begin the development phase. Depending on the
    complexity of the website, you can choose to code it from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, or
    utilize a CMS like WordPress or Drupal. Develop and integrate the required functionalities, such as contact forms,
    e-commerce capabilities, or third-party integrations.

  • Testing and Quality Assurance:

    Thoroughly test the website's functionality, responsiveness,
    and compatibility across different devices, browsers, and screen sizes. Check for any bugs, broken links, or performance issues.
    Conduct user testing to gather feedback and make necessary improvements.

  • Deployment and Launch:

    Prepare the website for deployment by configuring domain settings,
    setting up hosting, and transferring files to the live server. Perform a final check to ensure all elements are
    in place and the website is ready for launch. Make the website publicly accessible and monitor its performance.

  • Post-Launch Support and Maintenance:

    Offer post-launch support to the company, addressing any
    issues or concerns that arise. Provide training if needed, so they can manage and update the website themselves.
    Regularly update the website's software, plugins, and security measures to maintain its functionality and security.
